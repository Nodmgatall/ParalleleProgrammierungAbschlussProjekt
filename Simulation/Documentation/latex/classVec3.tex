Static method to calculate and return a vector which is the cross product of two vectors

Note\+: The cross product is simply a vector which is perpendicular to the plane formed by the first two vectors. Think of a desk like the one your laptop or keyboard is sitting on. If you put one pencil pointing directly away from you, and then another pencil pointing to the right so they form a \char`\"{}\+L\char`\"{} shape, the vector perpendicular to the plane made by these two pencils points directly upwards.

Whether the vector is perpendicularly pointing \char`\"{}up\char`\"{} or \char`\"{}down\char`\"{} depends on the \char`\"{}handedness\char`\"{} of the coordinate system that you\textquotesingle{}re using.

Static method to calculate and return the scalar dot product of two vectors

Note\+: The dot product of two vectors tell us things about the angle between the vectors. That is, it tells us if they are pointing in the same direction (i.\+e. are they parallel? If so, the dot product will be 1), or if they\textquotesingle{}re perpendicular (i.\+e. at 90 degrees to each other) the dot product will be 0, or if they\textquotesingle{}re pointing in opposite directions then the dot product will be -\/1.
